# The Autonomous Quote-to-Cash

**Sales · Contracts · Revenue · The Autonomous Enterprise Platform**

### The quote-to-cash chain that runs itself — reasons, acts, and accounts for itself.

The Autonomous Quote-to-Cash is the founding process pillar of The Autonomous Enterprise Platform — an explainability-first, EU AI Act-compliant architecture for ClaraVis Medical Systems. It governs the full commercial lifecycle from inbound inquiry through configuration, contract, revenue recognition, and installed-asset intelligence, each with an explicit EU AI Act risk classification and a named human checkpoint.

---

## What It Does

Eight modules, numbered M-01 through M-08 in this pillar's original build sequence — four are this pillar's own commercial and asset-intelligence agents; four are platform-wide governance crown modules that happen to share this pillar's numbering:

| Module | What It Does | Risk Classification |
|---|---|---|
| **M-01 · CCAI Sales Agent** | Handles inbound MRI/CT inquiry qualification autonomously through turn 11, then escalates to a Senior AE with a full briefing. | EU AI Act — Limited Risk |
| **M-02 · ContractGuard** | Reads and risk-scores 200+ contract clause types via Gemini 1.5 Pro's full-document context window, routing non-standard terms to Legal. | EU AI Act — High Risk |
| **M-03 · RevRec AI** | Classifies ASC 606 / IFRS 15 revenue recognition (lease vs. sale, multi-element arrangements) before any GL entry posts, with Finance Controller HITL. | EU AI Act — High Risk |
| **M-05 · Asset IQ** | Predicts Remaining Useful Life across the 12,000+ unit installed base from unified telemetry, replacing reactive maintenance with predictive scheduling. | EU AI Act — High Risk |

Four further modules — **M-04 FinRisk Sentinel, M-06 GreenOps Platform, M-07 Strategy Dashboard, M-08 Data Governance** — were numbered within this pillar's original build sequence but operate as platform-wide governance crown modules from the start. Data Governance (M-08) is the sole Horizon 1 module every other module in every pillar depends on before reaching production.

---

## Why This Pillar Exists

ClaraVis's quote-to-cash process runs through 9 sequential handoffs across Sales, Applications Engineering, Legal, Service, Finance, Revenue Recognition, Logistics, and Post-Sales — each a human, an email, and a 3–5 day delay, averaging a 47-day CPQ cycle with no end-to-end owner. Three existing predictive models (revenue recognition, asset failure prediction, contract risk scoring) are EU AI Act Annex III high-risk and were flagged non-compliant in a Q3 2025 audit — none produce a human-reviewable explanation or have a documented oversight checkpoint. The 12,000+ unit installed base reports DICOM service events to six regional systems with no common schema, leaving predictive maintenance impossible — the resulting €40M annual warranty over-reserve exists because nobody can predict failures, so Finance provisions for the worst case every quarter. ContractGuard and RevRec AI carry High Risk classification because they materially influence contract terms and financial reporting — both operate under explicit Legal and Finance Controller HITL checkpoints rather than autonomous authority.

## Where This Sits in the Platform

Quote-to-Cash's Financial Risk signal feeds the governance-layer FinRisk Sentinel as one of three contributing pillars, alongside Procure-to-Pay and Finance Operations. RevRec AI (M-03) executes inside this pillar, but its compliance governance and audit ownership belong to Finance Operations — the Finance Controller is the named HITL approver, the same pattern as ContractGuard running here with Legal as the approving authority. Asset IQ's telemetry signal is the direct sibling of Supply Chain's InventoryOrchestrator and DemandIQ — both read from physical-asset and field signal, at different points in the value chain.
